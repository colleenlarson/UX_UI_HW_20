TRADING ACES Mobile App

The challenge was to design a mobile app that solves a real need in a user's everyday life. Our team created "Trading Aces" that provides a digital space for people to trade their skills and services within their communities without spending money.

My Role

Our team consisted of 3 designers and Trading Aces was my brainchild that inspired the app idea.

Tools

Miro, Figma, Marvel App, G-Suite, Trello Board

Project Overview

Our team conducted user research to gauge the interest for an app that creates a digital space to trade services with others. We knew that initial research would be neccessary to understand the interest in this app and to provide design guidance.

User Research

A testing plan was created and I recorded 3 user interviews and synthesized the data from the google survey.

5 Qualitative User Interviews
18 Respondents - Quantitative Survey

Definition & Ideation

The Problem

Not everyone has the financial resources to pay for services when they need it.

Insight Statement

Individuals need a space to share and trade services so they don't have to pay for needed services.

I created an empathy map to help understand our users pain points of wanting to safely trade their skills and feel connected to their communities while saving money.

Prototyping & Testing

Findings and Iterations

Our team tested the lo-fi prototype to determine the usability by testing onboarding, earning tradebucks, redeeming tradebucks for a specific trade, and posting trades. My role was to combine the results from the usability testing so our team could discuss necessary iterations on our prototype in all categories tested. Ratings, review links, and a tutorial will need to be added to the mockup design.
